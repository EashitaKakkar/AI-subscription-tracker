# Growth & Optimization Strategy

For a B2B lead-generation tool like **SpendLens**, traditional consumer metrics like Daily Active Users (DAU) are misleading. Since a founder or manager only audits their stack during quarterly reviews or budget cuts, we focus on **Conversion Efficiency** and **Lead Quality**.

---

## The North Star Metric: "High-Intent Lead Conversion Rate"
**Definition:** The percentage of unique visitors who complete the audit and successfully trigger the "Email Report" action with a valid business domain.

**Why this matters:** Our primary goal is not just to show people a dashboard, but to capture qualified leads for Credex infrastructure credits. A high conversion rate proves the "Value-First" journey is compelling enough for a busy founder to trade their professional email address for the AI-generated insights.

---

##  3 Input Metrics (The Drivers)

1.  **Audit Completion Rate (Funnel Depth):** The ratio of users who start Step 1 (Tool Selection) vs. those who reach the final "Savings Hero" screen. This measures if the UI/UX is intuitive enough to keep a user engaged through the manual input of their tech stack.
2.  **Average Identified Savings per Audit:** The dollar amount of waste detected by the Audit Engine. If this number is too low, the perceived value of the tool drops, and users are less likely to share it or engage with the follow-up email.
3.  **Viral Share Coefficient (K-Factor):** The number of unique views generated by each "Shareable URL" created via the Supabase backend. This drives organic growth without increasing ad spend.

---

##  First Instrumentation Priorities
Before scaling, I would instrument **"Form Drop-off Heatmaps."** Specifically, I want to track which step (e.g., inputting seat counts vs. selecting tools) causes the most friction. Using a tool like PostHog or LogRocket would allow us to see if users are getting stuck on specific tool tiers or if the emoji-based tool selection is confusing.

---

##  The Pivot Trigger: "The < $200 Threshold"
If the **Median Identified Savings** per audit falls below **$200/year**, we trigger a pivot decision.

**The Reasoning:** If the tool consistently finds negligible savings, the "pain point" we are solving isn't sharp enough to justify a lead-gen funnel. 

**The Pivot Plan:** 
*   **Direction A:** Move "Up-market" to focus strictly on Enterprise SaaS (e.g., Salesforce, AWS) where the waste is higher.
*   **Direction B:** Shift from a "Savings Tool" to a "Tech Stack Benchmarking Tool," where the value is seeing how your team's spend compares to other startups of the same size.
